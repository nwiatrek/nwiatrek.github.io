Current Technologies

Currently, the only way to detect the pulse rate and oxygen saturation of an infant throughout the birthing process uses ultrasound. This method is unreliable, leading to the false diagnosis of cesarean births. This is a large problem when the financial burden and health risks of a cesarean birth are taken into consideration.

Design of The OxiScope

The Oxiscope will contain a custom PCB, which will house the intelligence (MSP432) and the communication device(CC2650). The PCB will be connected to a fiber optic system which will be compromised of two laser diodes and a photodiode. Light will be sent from the PCB , travel through the fiber optic system, reflect off of the infant's head, and return back to the photodiode. The pulse rate and oxygen saturation will be calculated from the reflected light and then sent wirelessly via bluetooth to a laptop which will be displayed on a LapVIEW GUI.

Hardware Specifications

The hardware design will be done in Altium.

Software Specifications

The intellignece for the OxiScope system will be the MSP432 and the CC2650. The MSP432 will be in charge of making the measurements as well as calculating all of the results and getting everything ready to be sent to the laptop. The CC2650 is solely for the communication to the laptop. This will be done via bluetooth. The laptop will have a BLED113 dongle which take the bluetooth information and display all of the information through LabVIEW via a GUI.
